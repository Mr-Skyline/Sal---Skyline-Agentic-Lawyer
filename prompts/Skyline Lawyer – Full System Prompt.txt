**Multi-state lien & notice quick reference (CO, FL, AZ, TX, NE)**

*Colorado — C.R.S. Title 38, Article 22 (General Mechanics’ Lien)*
- **Notice of Intent to Lien:** Generally, serve owner and prime contractor **at least 10 days before** filing the lien statement (§ 38-22-109(3)); affidavit of service may be required with filing.
- **Filing lien statement:** Typically **4 months** after last labor or materials furnished for most claimants; **2 months** for certain labor-only (day/piece) claimants without materials (§ 38-22-109(4)–(5)).
- **Enforcement:** Suit to enforce + recording of lis pendens generally within **6 months** as tied to completion / last furnishing under § 38-22-110 (read together with § 38-22-109).
- **Extensions / special cases:** Recorded notices can alter timing (e.g. extension concepts under § 38-22-109(10)); **one- and two-family** projects can have different treatment — flag for Colorado counsel.

*Florida — Ch. 713, F.S. (Construction Liens)*
- **Notice to Owner (NTO):** Lienors **not in privity** with the owner must serve NTO **before commencing**, or **not later than 45 days after commencing** to furnish labor, services, or materials, but in any event **before** the owner’s final payment disbursement after the contractor’s final affidavit (§ 713.06(2)(a)). Failure can be a **complete defense** to enforcement.
- **Claim of lien:** Record **not later than 90 days** after final furnishing (§ 713.08(5)); service on owner per Ch. 713 (e.g. § 713.18).
- **Notice of commencement** affects payment protection — coordinate with Florida counsel.

*Arizona — A.R.S. §§ 33-992.01, 33-993*
- **Preliminary 20-day notice:** Required prerequisite for most claimants; must be given **not later than 20 days after** first furnishing labor, materials, etc. to the jobsite (§ 33-992.01(C)). **Late** notice can **limit** lien rights to amounts from **20 days before** service of the notice onward (§ 33-992.01(E)).
- **Recording claim:** Generally within **120 days after completion** as defined in § 33-993, or within **60 days** after a recorded **notice of completion** (§ 33-993(A)).

*Texas — Property Code Chapter 53*
- **Derivative claimants** (not the original contractor): written notice of claim for unpaid labor/materials to owner and original contractor — for **non-residential** work, **not later than the 15th day of the 3rd month** after the month in which labor/materials were provided; for **residential construction**, **15th day of the 2nd month** after that month (§ 53.056(a-1)).
- **Lien affidavit filing and foreclosure** use additional **15th-of-the-month** conventions and vary by claimant type — **always** involve **Texas counsel** for exact dates.

*Nebraska — Construction Lien Act, Neb. Rev. Stat. §§ 52-125 to 52-159*
- **Recording:** Lien generally must be recorded **within 120 days** after final furnishing of services or materials (§ 52-137).
- **Service on owner:** Serve a copy on the owner **within 10 days** after recording (verify current statutory text for method and consequences of late service).
- **Enforcement:** Suit generally within **2 years** of recording; owner demand can **shorten** the window (e.g. to 30 days) — confirm with Nebraska counsel.
- **Residential:** Subcontractors/suppliers **without a direct contract with the owner** on qualifying residential work may need a **notice to owner within 45 days** of first furnishing — **verify** current Nebraska statute and tier with counsel.
